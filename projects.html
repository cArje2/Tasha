2025

Academic Youth Empowerment

In February 2025, Wezesha Youth Initiative hosted an educational outreach event at Maono High School in Kibra,Nairobi. Our main aim was to have a forum that will involve discussing the various challenges faced by youth in slum communities. We divided the students into two groups.The boys were with one of our male facilitator and the ladies with our female facilitators.The students were very open with us and we got to understand the various challenges they face in the slums which also end up leading to early teenage pregnancies, drug and substance abuse and school dropouts.This helped us to formulate different action plans that will enable us to address these issues.

2025

International Women's Day

On March 8, 2025 we organized an auspicious event to celebrate women, the day being International Women’s Day. It was not just an ordinary event, but a wake up call to women to rise and take spaces and be able to improve their lives.The event was opened with heartfelt words from the chairperson. We later had various stakeholders from different organizations giving speeches on the impact of women in the society. Afterwards, we had our speakers addressing different topics like mental wellness and financial literacy. The day ended with us gifting the women. The success of the event was made possible through the generous support of partners, well-wishers, and Wezesha members who contributed financially and volunteered their time.

About

History

Wezesha Youth Initiative was born out of a deep need to respond to the challenges faced by young people in Kibra. Kibra is one of the largest slums in Kenya.It has an estimated population of about two million people. The people living in this slum face various challenges like poor sanitation,unemployment,crime just to mention a few.The youths who are the most face various challenges like early teenage pregnancies ,drug and substance abuse,school dropouts and unemployment.It is because of this that Wezesha was born to be able to help improve the lives of these youths and to ultimately make our community a better place.. The name “Wezesha,” which means “to enable” in Swahili, reflects the heart of the initiative—to create opportunities where there were none, and to give hope where it had been lost. The organization was established with a clear purpose: to empower young people, especially those who had dropped out of school due to poverty, early pregnancies, lack of mentorship, or other social barriers. From its early days, Wezesha focused on practical solutions such as school reintegration programs for teenage mothers, scholarship sourcing for vulnerable students, mental health awareness, financial literacy training, and skill building workshops.Its commitment to long-term impact continues to inspire hope and transformation.
